A Little Bit About Sydney, Australia:
Every great country has its hub: a place, either large or small, where the world just seems a little closer. Sydney is Australia's oldest and largest city, founded in 1788 by Captain Arthur Phillip.

Sydney has always been a city fascinated with sports. The interest of Sydney's residents in sport will be a great asset at the 2000 Olympic Games. Many of the Australian spectators would have tried their hand at the Olympic sports being performed. They will appreciate all sports and cheer for all athletes who strive to achieve their best.

When Sydney was awarded the 2000 Olympic Games in 1993, there was a developmental boom. This boom led to the design and construction of a billion-dollar building known as the Olympic Stadium; as well as adjacent venues at Sydney Olympic Park, Homebush Bay. Other venues include the Sydney Convention Center, where Weightlifting contests will held. Competitors will meet in the convention center, which is located at Darling Harbour. It has the capacity to hold 3800 spectators.
