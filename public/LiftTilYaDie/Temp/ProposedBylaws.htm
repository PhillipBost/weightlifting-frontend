Tommie,

Thank you for giving us the time to review and comment on the proposed USAW Bylaws. I will try to make my comments on the proposed Bylaws in the order they appear, although at times I may expand that and/or deviate a bit.

SECTION 5. MEMBERS
Section 5.1. Categories of Membership.
b. Technical Members  Ends with "Any coach interested in being in this category must have achieved at least a level three coaching certification from USAW".

I believe that a similar requirement be place on Officials or Referees by saying

Any Official or Referee interested in being in this category must have achieved at least a USAW National Referee.

d. Olympic Members. Those eligible for USAW membership in this category shall be any individual who has won a Summer Olympic Games medal(s) for the United States in the sport of weightlifting. Such persons shall be granted a lifetime membership in USAW without the requirement to pay dues.

Since Weightlifting is a Summer Sport, this is redundant. Should Weightlifting become a Winter Sport, this would have to be changed. So why put it in?

Section 5.2. Voting Members.
b. All members shall have voting privileges.... In order to be eligible to vote in an election, an individual shall only be allowed to vote in one (1) membership category; must be at least eighteen (18) years of age; and must be a member of USAW, with fully paid dues, sixty (60) days prior to the date of an election. For purposes of clarification, any individual who is a member of more than one (1) membership category shall designate the membership category in which he or she shall vote.
 
Notwithstanding these restrictions on voting, membership in USAW is open to individuals who are less than eighteen (18) years of age; however, they shall not be allowed to vote in a USAW election.

I have 2 items highlighted here because I believe there are 2 problems:
1. At the 2002 Board of Governors (BOG) Meeting, the membership voted down a proposal to limit voting to those 18 & Older. Also, would that be 18 in actual age or in Weightlifting age? In Weightlifting, all Athletes are considered the age they turn anytime during a calendar year.

Apparently this last part was answered below in the USOC AAC Election procedures where it says:

eighteen (18) years of age or older by December 31 of the year in which the election is held.

IF we leave the 18 age minimum, this language should also be added here.

2. 60 day membership seems to be an arbitrary number. Assuming that future votes will be eMail and/or SnailMail in nature, it would seem to mean that the "Date of an Election" is more a voting period, than a date. Even if we stick with the 60 day rule, shouldn't then read "sixty (60) days prior to the END date of an election" or some such language?

SECTION 6. BOARD OF DIRECTORS
Section 6.4. Qualifications
Prior to serving as a USAW director, if not already a member, an individual must become a member of USAW.

Question: If you have to be a member for 60 days to vote, how long before do you have to be a member to serve?

Section 6.6. Election/Selection.

This section is the first appearance of the concept of the Nominating & Governance Committee (NGC), so I want to comment on it here. I was first exposed to the USOC's idea of the NGC when I served on the USOC AAC and Board of Directors (BOD) from Jan. 1989 until Jan. 1993. I immediately realized that it wasn't a true Nominating Committee, but really a "Coronating" Committee. By screening applicants and only nominating a bare minimum, the USOC's Committee made sure they decided who would get elected, which meant that those that controlled the NGC, would control the USOC. This would be the same story in USAW.

I believe that the NGC should be that of a "Verification" Committee. In other words, if 5, 10 or 1000 people submit their names as candidates for a position, the NGC should only verify that they did meet the criteria for election to that position and make no judgments on those applying to run. This is the only way to be fair to the membership and all those wanting to serve.

ii Technical Director - As I mentioned above, if there is a minimum of Level 3 for Coaches, there should be a minimum of National Referee for Officials/Referees.

Section 6.15. Regular and Special Meetings
Special meetings of the Board may be called by the Chairman of the Board, and must also be called when requested in writing by one-third (1/3) or more of the members of the Board.

Our current Bylaws also allow for a Special Meeting to be called for by "one-fourth (1/4) of the Board of Governors". I understand that these proposed Bylaws doesn't have a body equal to the BOG, but there should be a way the Membership can "make" the BOD have a meeting. Surely, the problems of this past BOD are examples of areas where 1/4 of the membership may have agreed the BOD needed to act (differently).

SECTION 7. OFFICERS
Section 7.2. Election/Selection.
The Chair of the Board shall be elected from among the directors of the Board by majority vote.

I believe that it would be better if the Membership directly elected the Chair. This would help ensure that the BOD is lead by a person that a majority of the Membership supported. With the proposed elections by segments, it is possible that the BOD be made up of 10 Directors that only have support from their particular segment. Choosing a Chair from that BOD could mean the Chair could have less than 20% support of the Membership. Surely not a good situation.

Section 7.3. Tenure.
The term of office of the Chair of the Board shall be two (2) years.

I'm still not convinced that having a staggered BOD is a good idea. It means that every 2 years, new Directors have to be brought up to speed. I believe the possibility of changing every 2 years is even worse of an idea for the Chair. The Chair should sit for 4 years!


Section 7.5. Restrictions.
Officers of USAW shall perform their functions with due care. No individual may serve simultaneously as an officer of USAW and as an officer of an organization holding membership in USAW, or as an officer of another amateur sports organization that is recognized by the USOC as an NGB.

Since Clubs are "organizations holding membership in USAW", this would mean Club Officers wouldn't be allowed to be Officers. This needs to be fixed!

SECTION 8. COMMITTEES
Section 8.5. Term Limits.
No committee member shall serve for more than three (3) consecutive terms.

Can a person serve an unlimited number of 3 consecutive terms as long as they take 1 term off in between? If they can, why bother limiting it? If it's more than 1 term off in between, how long is it?

Section 8.12. Nominating and Governance Committee.
a. The initial Nominating and Governance Committee shall consist of the following five (5) individuals: three (3) individuals appointed by the USOC, one (1) Elite Athlete, as that term is defined by the USOC, elected by members that are classified as Elite Athletes and one (1) current member of USAW who is not a current member of the board selected by the current Board of Directors.

This make up is totally unacceptable! The USOC has had 40% of the members of the group that created these new Bylaws. Now the USOC wants to have 60% CONTROL of the initial NGC. This is too much! It also gives the current BOD power to select a member as well. This year's BOG voted down the idea of the BOD having any say in this Bylaws Committee and as a BOG member, I don't think they should have any say on the first NGC either. At a minimum, the first NGC should be created using the same make up that was used for the Bylaws Committee: 2 from the USOC, 2 from the BOG & 1 Athlete. I personally would prefer more autonomy and have it 4 from the BOG and 1 Athlete. Or even 1 USOC, 3 BOG and 1 Athlete. BUT to give the USOC control of the NGC, especially if it remains a Coronating Committee is not a good way to get the membership behind the new Bylaws and processes.

e. The Nominating and Governance Committee shall:
i. identify and evaluate prospective candidates for the Board;
ii. select individuals to serve on the Board as provided in these Bylaws;
iii. recommend as requested by the Board, individuals to serve on various committees and task forces;
iv. consult with the Ethics Committee with respect to vetting all nominations for potential conflicts of interest or other problematic background issues;
v. develop and recommend to the Board for its consideration an annual self-evaluation process of the Board and its committees and task forces; and
vi. select all nominees to be put forward for positions on the IWF Executive Committee, Medical Committee, Scientific and Research Committee and Technical Committee.
vii. perform such other duties as assigned by the Board.

The following needs to be added (and not necessarily in "last place"):

viii accept nominations from the membership, verify eligibility of those nominated by the membership and put forward ALL eligible candidates for all positions.

This would change the function from coronating candidates to actually nominating them.

SECTION 10. USAW ATHLETES’ ADVISORY COUNCIL
Section 10.3. Election/Selection.
Seven (7) individuals

7 members is being overly optimistic. I've been to most Athletes meeting for the last 30 years and turn out is proportional to the issues discussed. But even in years when there have been major problems, it is hard to get more than 5 candidates for any position. And it's even harder to get Athletes to serve on the various committees. So it should be changed to a total of 5. 1 with the highest votes, 1 each male and female with the next highest votes for their gender and the USOC AAC Rep and their Alternate. Since the USOC AAC Rep and Alternate have to be of opposite genders, this still gives the same gender mix as the one proposed.


Section 10.6. Chair.
...The term of office of the USAW Athletes’ Advisory Council Chair shall be four years.

I find it interesting that the Athlete Chair is for 4 years yet the BOD Chair is only 2. Let's have a little consistency and make both a 4 year term (as I mentioned above).

SECTION 11. USOC ATHLETES’ ADVISORY COUNCIL
Section 11.3. Election/Selection.
After an individual has received ten (10) nominations, mailed the signatures of those that nominated the individual to the CEO, and the CEO has verified the authenticity and eligibility of those that nominated the individual, then the CEO shall place the individual on the ballot for election to the USOC Athletes’ Advisory Council.

This is the language that should be used for the NGC. A person should only have to submit their signed petition to the NGC and the NGC would then "Verify the Authenticity and eligibility of those nominated" and then place ALL on the ballot.

SECTION 13. CHIEF EXECUTIVE OFFICER (CEO)

Section 13.3. Secretary General.
The CEO shall serve as Secretary General of USAW and in this capacity shall represent USAW in relations with the IWF.

This sounds like it would mean that the CEO needs to be "Weightlifting" person. That would tie our hands from hiring a person that is an outsider, but a Sports CEO type (University Athletic Director, College Conference Executive, Sports Marketer, etc.).  Unless all they do is get the mail from the IWF and pass it on to the BOD, this is the wrong person for this job.

SECTION 15. SANCTIONING EVENTS
Section 15.1. Prompt Review of Request.
USAW shall promptly review every request submitted by an amateur sports organization or person for a sanction and make a determination on such request to (a) hold an international or national amateur athletic competition in the United States; or (b) to sponsor United States Weightlifting athletes to compete in an international athletic competition held outside the United States.

WHERE IS THE SANCTIONING OF LOCAL EVENTS? The Bylaw Template had:

ARTICLE 6. REGIONAL DIVISIONS AND ASSOCIATION OF STATE ORGANIZATIONS 

Why are these, or more importantly Local Weightlifting Committees (LWC), not included? How are we to have non National/International Competitions? I understand that the LWCs evolved out of the old AAU District structure and this Bylaws process is an attempt to wipe out the past. But for our Sport, LWCs work!. We had Regions when we were part of the AAU, but after we broke away from the AAU, they died mostly due to lack of interest. And State Organizations are too Big for some States and too Small for others. Here in California, we have 2 LWCs (down from the AAU's 4 Districts), but they are run differently. So different that we can't even agree on what our State Records should be! That is why we need to keep the LWCs and maybe tweak them a little.
